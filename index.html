Plastic waste can cause damage to marine ecosystems, threaten the survival of marine species, and disrupt the overall environmental balance.

What happens when the sea is polluted by garbage?

Impact on human health

If we consume fish or marine animals contaminated with plastic waste, it can cause health problems in humans, such as poisoning or other diseases.

Disturbance to marine ecosystems

Plastic waste can disrupt the balance of marine ecosystems because many marine animals are trapped in plastic waste or eat it as food, which can cause death or disrupt their life cycle.

Economic damage

If the tourism industry, fishing or other sectors that depend on marine ecosystems are affected by plastic waste pollution, it can cause significant financial losses.

PROJECT 1:

OCEANBOUND CLEAN UP

PROBLEMS

The process of plastic recycling is inevitably accompanied by the production of plastic waste effluent, commonly referred to as "Mud". This byproduct is typically discharged into the wastewater, posing a serious risk to the environment. In an attempt to mitigate this issue, the "Mud" is often transported to the nearest landfill site, which is not the ideal solution for waste management.

SOLUTION

The "Mud," which is a byproduct of plastic recycling, can be transformed into useful building materials, specifically bricks, through a repurposing process. The composition of the "Mud" is ideal for creating durable and long-lasting bricks. Despite this, recycled plastic compound bricks are slightly pricier than conventional building bricks, but the Impac+ program provides incentives to encourage the reuse of "Mud" in brick production, making them competitive in the market. This strategy helps to promote sustainable building practices and reduce plastic waste effluent.

PROJECT 2:

FISHING NET RECOVERY

PROBLEMS

Discarded fishing gear like nets and ropes is a major threat to sea life when left in the ocean. Without proper incentives for recovery, this gear is often dumped in landfills or cut away at sea, contributing to the high amount of plastic pollution in the ocean.

SOLUTION

Our program aims to provide the necessary funds to encourage fishermen to collect and recycle their end-of-life fishing gear. By doing so, we create a viable pathway for the reclamation of these materials, which would otherwise end up polluting our oceans and contributing to the global plastic crisis.
